## DETAILED FINDINGS

### CLAIM 1: San Luis Potosí Metropolitan Population

**CLAIM:** "Population ~1,000,000 (metro)"

**VERDICT:** ⚠️ PARTIALLY TRUE

**INVESTIGATION SUMMARY:**
The figure understates the current metropolitan population of San Luis Potosí. Independent demographic trackers place the 2025 metro area above 1.3 million inhabitants.

**EVIDENCE CHAIN:**
- **Primary Source:** [INEGI — Panorama Sociodemográfico 2020](https://www.inegi.org.mx/) — Metro area already 1.22 million at 2020 census
- **Corroborating Source 1:** [Macrotrends San Luis Potosí Metro Population](https://www.macrotrends.net/cities/21866/san-luis-potosi/population) — 1,311,000 for 2025 (+1.47% YoY)
- **Corroborating Source 2:** [CityPopulation.de Metropolitan Areas Mexico](https://www.citypopulation.de/en/mexico/metro/A38__san_luis_potos%C3%AD/) — lists SLP among top-10 metro areas, well above 1 million

**DETAILED ANALYSIS:**
The "~1,000,000 metro" figure in the article is conservative and closer to the city-proper count rather than the full metropolitan zone (which includes Soledad de Graciano Sánchez). Updated sources report 1.3M+.

**CONFIDENCE:** High — Multiple statistical aggregators confirm.

### CLAIM 5: Rent — 1-Bedroom Apartment in City Center

**CLAIM:** "Monthly rent 1BR center: SLP $380–550 USD; SMA $900–1,500 USD"

**VERDICT:** ⚠️ PARTIALLY TRUE

**INVESTIGATION SUMMARY:**
The SMA range matches current Numbeo data. The SLP range is outdated — current listings in the historic center exceed the upper bound of the cited range.

**EVIDENCE CHAIN:**
- **Primary Source:** [Numbeo — San Miguel de Allende (Feb 2026)](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/San-Miguel-de-Allende) — 1BR center 20,908 MXN (~$1,230 USD)
- **Primary Source:** [Numbeo — San Luis Potosí (Dec 2025)](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/San-Luis-Potosi) — 1BR center 11,800 MXN (~$700 USD)
- **Corroborating Source:** [Expatistan — San Miguel de Allende (Mar 2026)](https://www.expatistan.com/cost-of-living/san-miguel-de-allende) — Mid-range rental pricing aligned with Numbeo

**DETAILED ANALYSIS:**
SLP's Numbeo page carries a "low number of contributors" note, and the $380–550 band likely captures peripheral/university-area rentals rather than premium centro addresses. The directional claim (SLP significantly cheaper) is correct, but a realistic 2026 SLP centro range should read roughly $500–750 USD.

**CONFIDENCE:** Medium — Rental data in secondary Mexican cities has fewer contributors and wider variance by neighborhood.

### CLAIM 6: "San Miguel de Allende is 40–55% More Expensive Than SLP"

**CLAIM:** "San Miguel de Allende is 40-55% more expensive than San Luis Potosí for a similar quality of life"

**VERDICT:** ⚠️ PARTIALLY TRUE

**INVESTIGATION SUMMARY:**
The overall direction is correct, but the magnitude is overstated on some line items and understated on others. Cross-referenced Numbeo/Expatistan data yields an aggregate 30–45% premium for SMA over SLP, not 40–55%.

**EVIDENCE CHAIN:**
- **Primary Source:** [Numbeo San Miguel de Allende](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/San-Miguel-de-Allende) — Single person excluding rent ~$853 USD
- **Primary Source:** [Numbeo San Luis Potosí](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/San-Luis-Potosi) — Equivalent basket ~$650–700 USD
- **Corroborating Source:** [Livingcost.org — San Miguel de Allende](https://livingcost.org/cost/mexico/san-miguel-de-allende) — Single person $1,858/mo

**DETAILED ANALYSIS:**
Using apples-to-apples 1BR rent + single-person expenses: SMA ≈ $2,050/mo; SLP ≈ $1,400/mo. That's a 46% SMA premium — at the top of the article's stated range. But for couples and larger housing, the gap narrows closer to 35–40%. The 49% headline differential is defensible only for specific single-professional scenarios, not as a blanket claim.

**CONFIDENCE:** Medium — Numbeo sample sizes limit precision for smaller Mexican cities.

### CLAIM 11: Expat Community Size (SMA 10–15k; SLP 2–3k)

**CLAIM:** "San Miguel expat population ~10,000-15,000; San Luis Potosí ~2,000-3,000"

**VERDICT:** ❓ UNVERIFIABLE

**INVESTIGATION SUMMARY:**
Mexico's INM (Instituto Nacional de Migración) does not publish residency statistics at the municipal level in a way that distinguishes "expats" from short-term residents. Estimates in expat press vary widely.

**EVIDENCE CHAIN:**
- **Reference Source:** Multiple expat lifestyle publications cite 10,000–20,000 foreign residents in SMA, but none cite INM data directly
- **Counter-Evidence:** [INEGI 2020 Census — foreign-born residents](https://www.inegi.org.mx/) only reports foreign-born, not legal-status expats, complicating verification
- **Context:** SLP's growing automotive FDI has attracted German, Japanese, Korean professional communities — but exact numbers are not officially published

**DETAILED ANALYSIS:**
The ranges quoted are reasonable order-of-magnitude estimates used commonly in expat media, but they lack a single authoritative government source. The claim should be treated as informed estimate, not verified statistic.

**CONFIDENCE:** Low — No primary-source headcount exists.

## PATTERN ANALYSIS

### ACCURACY PATTERNS

Structural facts (UNESCO dates, company presence, altitudes, airport routes, award status) are highly accurate. Where the article drifts toward imprecision is in **cost-of-living specifics** — particularly rent ranges for San Luis Potosí, which understate 2026 market conditions due to limited Numbeo contributors.

### BIAS INDICATORS

The article positions San Luis Potosí favorably, which reflects the publisher's editorial mission (San Luis Way is an SLP-focused outlet). The pro-SLP framing is disclosed implicitly through branding; cost figures appear chosen near the low end of defensible ranges, amplifying the perceived SLP value advantage. No evidence of manipulation — just selective framing within acceptable editorial bounds.

### SOURCING QUALITY

The article references Numbeo, Expatistan, International Living, and INEGI appropriately. A stronger citation practice (inline links to specific data pages rather than general mentions) would improve transparency.
