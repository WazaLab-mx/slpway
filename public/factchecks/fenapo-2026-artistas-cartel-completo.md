**DETAILED ANALYSIS:**

### CLAIM 11: Palenque Tickets Sold "Only" Through fenapo.mx and eticket.mx

**CLAIM:** "Where do I buy FENAPO 2026 Palenque tickets? Palenque tickets are sold only through the official channels: fenapo.mx and eticket.mx."

**VERDICT:** ❌ FALSE

**INVESTIGATION SUMMARY:**
Two separate official announcements from the San Luis Potosí state government's FENAPO portal (dated June 3 and June 5, 2026) name **slpfastticket.com** as "la plataforma oficial" for Palenque ticket sales, alongside the in-person Palenque box office (10:00–18:00). Neither official notice mentions fenapo.mx or eticket.mx as ticket-purchase channels. A direct check of the eticket.mx page that surfaces for "Palenque Feria Nacional Potosina" currently shows **"NO HAY EVENTOS DISPONIBLES PARA ESTA SELECCIÓN"** (no events available) — it is not an active, functioning point of sale for FENAPO 2026 as of this verification. fenapo.mx is a legitimate informational site (Patronato de la Feria Nacional Potosina) but no evidence was found that it directly processes ticket sales, and it could not be reached during this investigation to confirm.

**EVIDENCE CHAIN:**
- [Gobierno del Estado SLP — ARRANCA VENTA DE BOLETOS PARA EL PALENQUE FENAPO 2026 (2026-06-03)](https://fenapo.slp.gob.mx/noticias/2026/6/3/arranca-venta-de-boletos-para-el-palenque-fenapo-2026/) — names slpfastticket.com as the official platform; no mention of fenapo.mx or eticket.mx
- [Gobierno del Estado SLP — GOBIERNO ESTATAL MANTIENE DISPONIBLES BOLETOS PARA EL PALENQUE FENAPO 2026 (2026-06-05)](https://fenapo.slp.gob.mx/noticias/2026/6/5/gobierno-estatal-mantiene-disponibles-boletos-para-el-palenque-fenapo-2026/) — repeats slpfastticket.com as the sole official channel, warns explicitly against "páginas piratas" (fraudulent pages)
- [SLP Fast Ticket — Boletos Palenque Fenapo 2026 | Sitio Oficial](https://slpfasticket.com/) — self-identifies as the official ticketing site
- **Counter-check:** [eticket.mx — Palenque Feria Nacional Potosina listing](https://www.eticket.mx/eventos.aspx?idlugar=285) — returns "no events available," indicating it is not a live sales channel for this edition

**DETAILED ANALYSIS:**
This is the one claim in the article where the practical, actionable guidance given to readers does not match what the fair's own government organizers are telling the public. The state government has twice (June 3 and June 5) publicly and explicitly named a single official platform — slpfastticket.com — and warned about fraudulent/pirate pages, which makes it more consequential that the post is directing readers toward two URLs (fenapo.mx, eticket.mx) that are not confirmed as active points of sale, one of which (eticket.mx) currently shows no purchasable inventory at all. This should be corrected to reference slpfastticket.com and the physical box office.

**CONFIDENCE:** High — two independent, dated, official government press releases explicitly name a different platform, and the specific eticket.mx URL was checked directly and shows no live events.

## PATTERN ANALYSIS

### ACCURACY PATTERNS

The article is extremely accurate on hard, checkable facts that come directly from the state government's own May 27, 2026 cartel announcement: dates, artist names, venue assignments (Foro vs. Palenque), and the "free vs. ticketed" distinction were all verified as an exact match against primary Foro/Palenque lists pulled independently from Infobae and El Universal SLP. The one failure is in a *service-oriented* claim added by the blog itself — where to actually buy tickets — which is not sourced from the original cartel-announcement wave of coverage (all dated May 27) but appears to be either inferred or based on generic/outdated platform names rather than the government's subsequent (June 3/5) ticket-sales announcements.

### BIAS INDICATORS

No political or promotional bias detected. The post does not appear to favor any artist, promoter, or ticket vendor commercially; the fenapo.mx/eticket.mx error reads as an factual slip rather than deliberate promotion, since San Luis Way has no evident affiliation with either domain.

### SOURCING QUALITY

The post's own "Fuentes" section names Infobae, El Universal SLP, Milenio, and feriafenapo.com as its verification sources for the lineup — and this investigation's independent primary-source pulls from Infobae and El Universal SLP corroborate the lineup content in full. However, the sourcing gap is that the article's ticket-purchase guidance was evidently not re-verified against the government's later (June) ticket-sales rollout, which happened after the original cartel sources were published.

- Ticket pricing details (e.g., Julión Álvarez zone pricing, Grupo Firme general admission) were incidentally verified via secondary sources but are not claims made in the post's English body text and were not separately scored.
