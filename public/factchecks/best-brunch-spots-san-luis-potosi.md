## CRITICAL CORRECTIONS MADE

These errors were identified during fact-checking and corrected in the live article on April 24, 2026:

1. **Gorditas de Morales location CORRECTED** — Changed from "Calle Reforma in Barrio de San Miguelito" to **"Camino a la Presa de San José in the Loma de los Filtros / Morales corridor"**. The historical anchor is the Presa de San José weekend traffic, not the San Miguelito barrio. Calle Reforma exists and runs near San Miguelito but is not the gordita corridor.
2. **Fork Le Brunch Atelier phone CLARIFIED** — Changed from a single shared "+52 444 481 1996" to listing **+52 444 481 1996 (Centro / Galeana 443) and +52 444 817 0705 (Le Brunch Atelier, Olmos 145)** as separate lines.
3. **Oliva framing CORRECTED** — Tightened from "Mediterranean brunch" daily to **"Sunday-only brunch (9:00 AM – 2:00 PM); Tuesday–Saturday dinner only"**. Oliva is officially "Bistro de Desayuno y Cena."
4. **Café de olla framing TIGHTENED** — Reframed from "Potosino patrimony" to **"a generic Mexican tradition commonly served alongside Potosino brunch"**. Café de olla is pan-Mexican, not specifically Potosino.
5. **Hogaza listing FLAGGED with caveat** — Kept in the article with a note: "verify operating address via @hogaza.cafe Instagram bio before visiting." The IG handle exists but its San Luis Potosí location could not be independently confirmed in this round of verification.

Calle Reforma exists but borders San Miguelito; one isolated gordita vendor with a San Miguelito origin story exists, but the famous calle is in Morales / Loma de los Filtros.

**EVIDENCE CHAIN:**

## PATTERN ANALYSIS

### ACCURACY PATTERNS

Venue-level claims (existence, address, hours, signature dishes) are accurate across the article — every single featured venue verified as real and operating. Where the article drifted: (1) one significant geographic error on the Gorditas de Morales street and barrio identification, (2) a phone number shared across two Fork concepts that have separate numbers, (3) Oliva's daily-vs-Sunday brunch framing, and (4) "Potosino patrimony" overreach on café de olla. All four issues were corrected.

### BIAS INDICATORS

The article is a curated "best of" list with editorial swaps — three venues removed, three added. The fact-check confirms the swap was based on fit and freshness, not on closures. The remaining venues lean toward higher-end and Centro-adjacent options, consistent with the publisher's audience. No bias-driven misrepresentation detected.

### SOURCING QUALITY

The article includes inline mention of phones, addresses, and IG handles — strong transparency for a food guide. Adding direct links to the IG bios (especially for Hogaza, where the SLP attribution is unverified) would further strengthen trust.
