### CLAIM 3: Rojo Ocejo — "Una familia se ahorra más de 500 pesos"

**CLAIM:** "Según el presidente del Patronato, Fernando Rojo Ocejo, la gratuidad significa que una familia se ahorra más de 500 pesos frente a lo que pagaría en otras ferias del país por estacionamiento, entradas y juegos."

**VERDICT:** ⚠️ PARTIALLY TRUE

**INVESTIGATION SUMMARY:**
The attribution, the figure and the framing are all real: Fernando Rojo Ocejo, president of the fair's Patronato, stated families save more than 500 pesos ("ahorro superior a 500 pesos") versus other national fairs. The partial verdict is because the statement was made in a January 2026 press release that describes the ongoing FENAPO model (with 2025-edition context) rather than an announcement issued explicitly for the 2026 edition, which slightly strains the article's "only 2026-edition announcements" sourcing frame.

**EVIDENCE CHAIN:**
- **Primary Source:** [slp.gob.mx (Jan 12, 2026)](https://slp.gob.mx/noticias/2026/1/12/san-luis-potos%C3%AD-es-referente-nacional-con-la-gratuidad-de-la-fenapo/) — Rojo Ocejo quoted; savings "superior a 500 pesos"; comparison vs. Feria de León (parking ~200 MXN, entry 15 MXN/person, rides 50+ MXN)
- **Corroborating Source 1:** [Código San Luis — SLP referente nacional con la gratuidad de la FENAPO](https://www.codigosanluis.com/slp-referente-gratuidad-fenapo/) — Same statement republished
- **Corroborating Source 2:** [El Heraldo SLP (Jan 10, 2026)](https://elheraldoslp.com.mx/new/2026/01/10/fenapo-se-consolida-como-referente-nacional-con-experiencia-gratuita-para-familias/) — Same communiqué

**DETAILED ANALYSIS:**
The blog renders the quote faithfully ("más de 500 pesos" ≈ "ahorro superior a 500 pesos") and correctly names Rojo Ocejo and his role. The underlying comparison basket (León fair prices) is drawn from the source's own benchmarking. Because the January release illustrates the model with 2025-edition examples, this statement is best characterized as a January 2026 institutional statement about a policy that continues into 2026 — accurate in substance, marginally looser than the article's "2026-edition announcements only" framing.

**CONFIDENCE:** High on the quote/attribution; Medium on the "2026-edition announcement" framing.

### CLAIM 5: Announced Ride-Safety Measures — Operator Training, Random Inspections, Staff Background Checks

**CLAIM:** "Seguridad anunciada: capacitación de operadores, inspecciones aleatorias y verificación de antecedentes del personal."

**VERDICT:** ⚠️ PARTIALLY TRUE

**INVESTIGATION SUMMARY:**
Two of three items are verbatim from the source: the State Civil Protection Coordination (CEPC) will train ride operators ("capacitará a los operadores") and conduct random inspections ("inspecciones aleatorias"). The third item is a paraphrase: the source says "revisiones de los perfiles del personal" (staff profile reviews) plus toxicology tests — the blog's "verificación de antecedentes" (background checks) is a slightly stronger rendering than the source wording.

**EVIDENCE CHAIN:**
- **Primary Source:** [Potosinoticias (Jun 6, 2026)](https://potosinoticias.com/2026/06/06/fenapo-2026-contara-con-60-juegos-mecanicos-gratuitos/) — CEPC: operator training, random inspections, "revisiones de los perfiles del personal," toxicology tests, operational checks on attractions
- **Corroborating Source 1:** [Quadratín SLP](https://sanluispotosi.quadratin.com.mx/san-luis-potosi/ampliara-fenapo-oferta-de-juegos-mecanicos-gratuitos-en-2026/) — Safety/supervision measures via Protección Civil estatal

**DETAILED ANALYSIS:**
"Revisiones de perfiles del personal" plausibly encompasses background vetting, but "verificación de antecedentes" carries a specific connotation (criminal-record checks) the source does not literally state. The blog also omits the announced toxicology testing — an omission, not an error. Recommended fix: soften to "revisión de perfiles del personal" or add "y pruebas toxicológicas" to mirror the source exactly.

**CONFIDENCE:** High that measures were announced; Medium on the precise "background checks" wording.

### ACCURACY PATTERNS

This is the strongest-sourced FENAPO post audited to date. Every named figure (60 rides, +15, ~45 baseline, 500 pesos, ages 3–15/16–19, phone 444 129 0999 ext. 510, 21 Foro nights, Aug 7–30) matched its named source exactly. The article's discipline of labeling unannounced items as unannounced — instead of recycling prior-year data — eliminated the largest error class seen in competing FENAPO coverage.

### BIAS INDICATORS

The post inherits the state government's promotional framing (the gratuity narrative and "shorter lines" rationale originate in official communiqués), but it consistently attributes those statements to their sources rather than asserting them in the site's own voice, and it adds independent cautionary advice (crowd warnings for Katy Perry/Mötley Crüe/Bizarrap nights, Palenque alcohol notice). No material bias distortion detected.

### SOURCING QUALITY

Excellent: a dedicated Fuentes section names outlet and date for each fact cluster, and the two paraphrase-level imprecisions found (Claim 3 framing, Claim 5 "verificación de antecedentes" vs. "revisiones de perfiles") are wording-precision issues, not factual errors. Suggested micro-fixes: mirror the CEPC wording on staff vetting and date-stamp the Rojo Ocejo quote.
